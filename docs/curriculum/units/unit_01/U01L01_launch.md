# Lesson: Unit Launch - Why Balance Matters

## Phase 1: Entry
- **Goal:** Brief hook or activator.
- **Activity:** The Messy Notebook Challenge. Show an image of a disorganized pile of receipts and notes. Ask students: "If you were Sarah, how would you figure out if you're actually 'ahead' or 'behind' right now?"

## Phase 2: Intro
- **Goal:** Set the stage, introduce the narrative, and watch the launch video.
- **Narrative:** {{narrative_hook}}
- **Video:** {{assets.video}} - Discuss the three buckets Sarah needs to track: What she HAS (Assets), what she OWES (Liabilities), and what she's WORTH (Equity).

## Phase 3: Guided
- **Goal:** Teacher-led exploration or class discussion using the simulation.
- **Activity:** Open the startup balance simulation ({{assets.simulation}}). Walk through a few initial transactions and watch how the A=L+E equation reacts. "Why did the equation stay balanced even after we bought that laptop?"

## Phase 4: Independent
- **Goal:** Students work through a basic scenario or initial data exploration.
- **Activity:** Pair Sort. Give students cards (or digital equivalents) representing common items (Cash, Laptop, Loan from Bank, Savings, Credit Card Debt). In pairs, students sort these into the three buckets: Assets, Liabilities, or Equity.

## Phase 5: Checkpoint
- **Goal:** Exit ticket to verify initial understanding of the unit's core challenge.
- **Auto-Grade:**
  - Define Assets, Liabilities, and Equity in your own words.
  - Complete the core accounting equation check ({{auto_grade.questions.0.question}}).

## Phase 6: Reflection
- **Goal:** Connect back to the business challenge and "Why this matters."
- **Activity:** "Why does balance build trust with investors or banks? If Sarah's books didn't balance, would you lend her money?"

---

## Teacher Notes
- **Pedagogy:** Focus on conceptual understanding before diving into numbers. Use physical props (buckets or cards) if helpful.
- **Setup:** Ensure the launch video is ready and the simulation link is shared with students.
