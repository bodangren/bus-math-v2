# Curriculum Unit Design Contract

## Purpose

This document is the canonical lesson-design contract for instructional units. When an LLM authors or redesigns curriculum, it must follow this sequence unless an explicit curriculum exception is recorded in the active track spec.

Redesign order is mandatory:

1. Redesign Unit 1 against this contract.
2. Verify Unit 1 in docs, authored content, and runtime.
3. Use the accepted Unit 1 redesign as the exemplar before redesigning later units.

Do not move on to other units while Unit 1 is still drifting from this contract.

## Canonical 11-Lesson Sequence

### Lesson 1: Unit Launch

- Introductory video
- Introduce the unit problem
- Introduce normal or familiar solutions students already understand
- Business simulation that introduces the problem and possible solution paths without requiring prior unit knowledge
- Exit ticket assessment
- Reflection

### Lesson 2: Accounting Principle 1

- Introduce accounting principle 1
- `I do`: teacher presentation/modeling
- `We do`: guided practice
- `You do`: independent production
- Exit ticket assessment
- Reflection

### Lesson 3: Accounting Principle 2

- Introduce accounting principle 2
- `I do`: teacher presentation/modeling
- `We do`: guided practice
- `You do`: independent production
- Exit ticket assessment
- Reflection

### Lesson 4: Domain Concept 3

- Lesson 4 may be accounting or Excel depending on unit needs
- It must still follow the same six-part structure:
  - introduce the concept
  - `I do`
  - `We do`
  - `You do`
  - exit ticket assessment
  - reflection

### Lesson 5: Excel Concept 2

- Introduce Excel concept 2 in service of the unit's accounting/business goals
- `I do`: model concept 1 + concept 2 where appropriate
- `We do`
- `You do`
- Exit ticket assessment
- Reflection

### Lesson 6: Excel Concept 3

- Introduce Excel concept 3 in service of the unit's accounting/business goals
- `I do`: model concept 1 + concept 2 + concept 3 where appropriate
- `We do`
- `You do`
- Exit ticket assessment
- Reflection

### Lesson 7: Whole-Class Guided Project Build

- Introduce the synthesis of all accounting and Excel principles in the unit
- Provide one scaffolded spreadsheet assignment using the same dataset for the entire class
- Provide the task description
- Provide a foundational-proficiency how-to
- Provide a "polish" or advanced-proficiency how-to
- Exit ticket assessment
- Reflection

### Lesson 8: Group Project Build with New Dataset

- Each of six groups receives a new dataset for an assignment structurally similar to Lesson 7
- Provide a foundational-proficiency how-to for the group workbook
- Students build the group version of the product

### Lesson 9: Advanced Polish

- Continue the Lesson 8 group assignment
- Provide the "polish" or advanced-proficiency how-to
- Students improve clarity, professionalism, and communication quality

### Lesson 10: Presentation

- Students present the product to the class
- Presentation expectations, audience, and evidence requirements must be explicit

### Lesson 11: Summative Assessment

- Three assessment levels:
  - knowledge
  - understanding
  - application

## Authoring Requirements

Every unit redesign must explicitly specify:

- which accounting principles are introduced in Lessons 2-4
- which Excel concepts are introduced in Lessons 4-6
- the common class dataset used in Lesson 7
- the six group datasets used in Lesson 8
- the foundational-proficiency how-to for Lessons 7-8
- the advanced-proficiency or polish how-to for Lessons 7 and 9
- the presentation rubric or presentation success criteria for Lesson 10
- the knowledge/understanding/application split for Lesson 11

## Runtime and Publishing Requirements

- Lessons 1-7 must publish as six-phase lessons.
- Lessons 8-10 must publish as project-sequence lessons.
- Lesson 11 must publish as a three-level summative.
- The authored curriculum must make the dataset and workbook expectations visible in the lesson content, not only in private planning docs.

## LLM Guardrails

When an LLM proposes or writes curriculum:

- it must name the role of each lesson in the unit arc
- it must state the accounting principle or Excel concept introduced in that lesson
- it must identify the dataset/workbook used
- it must distinguish foundational guidance from polish guidance where required
- it must not treat project days as generic placeholder group work
- it must not start redesigning later units until the Unit 1 redesign is approved
