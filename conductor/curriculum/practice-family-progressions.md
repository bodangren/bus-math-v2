# Practice Family Pedagogical Progressions

This document defines the recommended sequencing for practice families across the curriculum. These progressions are designed to scaffold student learning from supported reasoning to independent application.

## Core Progressions

### F → C: Transaction Effects to Transaction Matrix

**Family F (Transaction Effects)** introduces the impact of individual transactions on the accounting equation. Students learn to trace debits and credits through the basic equation.

**Family C (Transaction Matrix)** extends this to multi-transaction scenarios where students must determine the net effect across several transactions.

- Scaffold: F provides single-transaction reasoning with explicit debit/credit labels
- Application: C requires synthesizing multiple transactions without explicit scaffolding
- When to use: F in guided practice, C in independent practice and assessment

### J → K: Adjustment Calculation to Adjustment Effects

**Family J (Adjusting Calculations)** teaches students to compute the correct adjustment amount given a deferral, accrual, or depreciation scenario.

**Family K (Adjustment Effects)** asks students to reason about what happens when an adjustment is missed or recorded incorrectly.

- Scaffold: J focuses on the arithmetic and journal entry mechanics
- Application: K requires understanding the downstream impact on financial statements
- When to use: J before K within the same unit, never reverse this order

### D/Q → E: Complete Subtotals to Construct from Scratch

**Family D (Statement Subtotals - Balance Sheet)** and **Family Q (Statement Subtotals - Income Statement)** provide partially completed statements where students fill in missing subtotals.

**Family E (Statement Construction)** requires students to construct a complete statement from raw account data.

- Scaffold: D/Q provide the statement skeleton and line items
- Application: E requires selecting and organizing accounts without a template
- When to use: D/Q in early practice, E in later independent practice

### Statement → Numeric Presentation: Scaffolded to Unscaffolded

For each statement family, start with the structured presentation before moving to numeric-only formats.

- Balance Sheet: Start with `balance-sheet` layout, then move to numeric summarization
- Income Statement: Start with `income-statement` layout, then move to `retail-income-statement`
- Equity Statement: Start with `equity-statement` layout, then move to numeric flow

### J → N: Compute Depreciation to Present on Balance Sheet

**Family J (Adjusting Calculations - Depreciation)** teaches students to compute depreciation expense using straight-line method.

**Family N (Depreciation Presentation)** requires students to present the depreciated asset on the balance sheet with accumulated depreciation and net book value.

- Scaffold: J focuses on the depreciation calculation formula
- Application: N connects the calculation to the balance sheet presentation
- When to use: J before N, typically within the same lesson or adjacent lessons

### H Simple → Complex: Journal Entry Complexity

**Family H (Journal Entry)** supports difficulty progression through entry complexity.

- Simple entries: 2-line entries (one debit, one credit)
- Medium entries: 3-4 line entries with multiple debits or credits
- Complex entries: 5+ line entries requiring careful account selection

The `lineComplexity` metadata in Family H definitions supports this progression:
- Level 1: Basic two-line entries (most guided practice)
- Level 2: Multi-line entries with clear account pairings
- Level 3: Complex entries requiring account selection from a bank

## Recommended Unit Sequencing

### Unit 1: Balance by Design
- A (Accounting Equation) → M (Normal Balance) → F (Transaction Effects)
- D (Statement Subtotals - Balance Sheet) → E (Statement Construction)

### Unit 2: Flow of Transactions
- C (Transaction Matrix) → H (Journal Entry)
- G (Trial Balance Errors)

### Unit 3: Statements in Balance
- J (Adjusting Calculations) → K (Adjustment Effects)
- Q (Statement Subtotals - Income Statement) → N (Depreciation Presentation)

### Unit 4-8: Extended Applications
- P (Merchandising Entries) - introduced in Unit 4
- L (Cycle Decisions) - introduced in Unit 5
- O (Statement Subtotals - Retail) - introduced in Unit 6

## Assessment Considerations

When building assessments:
1. Always include at least one scaffolded family (F, D, Q) before unscaffolded (C, E)
2. Never place K before J in the same assessment
3. Use H complexity levels to differentiate assessment tiers
4. Mix statement families (D, Q, N) to verify transfer of skills
